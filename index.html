These are images of the sun taken by satellite SDO from NASA .

Space weather is a branch of space physics and aeronomy concerned with the time varying conditions within the Solar System, including the solar wind, emphasizing the space surrounding the Earth, including conditions in the magnetosphere, ionosphere and thermosphere.

These are some videos from satellites of the sun, they use different colours of filter so that we can see different things. On the orange coloured sun the magnetic field is so much easier to see than on the red sun. This shows the activity of the sun better.

Space Weather is very high on the National Risk Register and therefore it is very important to find out more about the sun and how it affects us. The Met Office work with many other organizations to do this, NASA, NOAA, ESA and RAL space. With 24/7 forecasters the Met Office own the UK space weather contract and therefore they are responsible for trying to understand it. There are many different organizations involved in Space Weather for example The Met Office, NOAA, NASA, ESA and UKSA all of which share data and information so that globally there is a much better way of reducing the impacts of Space Weather.
